The flagship

A CRM shouldn't just store your leads.
It should work them.

A lead lands at 3am. By 3:15 it's in your pipeline, matched to a company, and answered with a real email that asks the right next question. You wake up to a conversation that's already moving instead of a to-do list.

  1. 3:02am It arrives
    Dana Reyes · Ironwood Mfg Quote for 20 seats? Hi, we're outgrowing our current setup and need pricing for about 20 people…

    Email, website chat, or an ad lead form. Every channel lands in the same place.

  2. 3:02am It gets understood
    AI triage Lead High urgency Wants pricing · 20 seats Spam? No

    Read, classified, and summarized. Junk and bad actors get filtered out before they reach you.

  3. 3:03am It becomes real work
    Deal created: Ironwood Mfg Contact matched: Dana Reyes Timeline note written

    A lead becomes a deal. A support request becomes a ticket. Companies and contacts link themselves.

  4. 3:14am It gets answered
    Re: Quote for 20 seats? Hi Dana, happy to help. Two quick things so I can price this properly: are all 20 users full-time, and do you need the field service module?… Sent · 12 min after it landed

    A real reply that moves the deal forward. Review it first, or let it send on its own after a window you control.

It follows up so you don't have to. Prospect goes quiet? A polite nudge goes out on your schedule, and cancels itself the moment they reply.
Support threads keep themselves. Replies land on the same ticket, in the notes, with the status moved for you. No duplicates, no lost threads.
You're always in the driver's seat. Approve every message, or let them fly and keep a veto window. Nothing sends that you can't see and stop.
